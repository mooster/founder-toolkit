# Pitch Deck Scoring Rubric

Detailed criteria for each of the 10 review dimensions.

## 1. Narrative Arc (Does the story flow?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Compelling arc: problem → solution → why now → why you. Each slide builds on the last. |
| 4 | Clear flow with minor gaps. One slide feels out of place. |
| 3 | Logical but not compelling. Information is there but doesn't build momentum. |
| 2 | Disjointed. Jumps between topics. Reader loses the thread. |
| 1 | No discernible narrative. Random collection of slides. |

**Common mistake:** Jumping to the product before establishing the problem. The audience must feel the pain before seeing the solution.

## 2. Problem Statement (Do I feel the pain?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Visceral problem with quantified impact. Clear who suffers and how much. |
| 4 | Real problem, mostly quantified. Could use one more data point. |
| 3 | Problem exists but feels theoretical. No quantification. |
| 2 | Describes a feature gap, not a business problem. |
| 1 | No clear problem, or manufactured problem that doesn't resonate. |

**Common mistake:** "There's no good tool for X" is a feature gap. "Companies lose $Y million annually because X" is a problem.

## 3. Market Size (Is this a big opportunity?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Bottoms-up sizing. Clear path: X companies × $Y ACV = $Z SAM. Sources cited. |
| 4 | Mix of top-down and bottoms-up. SAM is reasonable. |
| 3 | Top-down only ("$500B global market") but SAM is defined. |
| 2 | Just TAM with no SAM/SOM. "The market is huge." |
| 1 | No sizing, or obviously inflated numbers with no methodology. |

**Common mistake:** If SAM equals TAM, the analysis is not credible. Also: citing analyst reports without explaining the capture path.

## 4. Product / Solution (Does this actually solve it?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Demo or clear workflow shown. Obvious connection to the problem. Differentiated approach. |
| 4 | Clear solution with screenshots. Connection to problem is mostly clear. |
| 3 | Solution described but not shown. Feature list without value framing. |
| 2 | Vague description. Sounds like 10 other products. |
| 1 | No clear solution, or solution doesn't address the stated problem. |

**Common mistake:** Feature list instead of value proposition. "We have real-time analytics, AI-powered insights, and a dashboard" tells nothing about the unique value.

## 5. Traction (Proof it works)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Revenue + growth rate + retention + logos. Multiple proof points. |
| 4 | Revenue with growth, or strong user metrics with clear monetization path. |
| 3 | Some users or pilots, limited revenue data. |
| 2 | Vanity metrics only (downloads, signups without activation). |
| 1 | No traction data, or data is misleading. |

**Stage calibration:** A pre-seed deck with 3 pilot customers and a letter of intent can score 4/5. A Series A deck with the same traction scores 2/5.

## 6. Business Model (How does this make money?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear pricing, demonstrated unit economics, path to profitability. |
| 4 | Pricing model clear, unit economics estimated with stated assumptions. |
| 3 | Revenue model described but no unit economics. |
| 2 | "SaaS subscription" with no specifics on pricing or margins. |
| 1 | No business model, or "we'll figure out monetization later." |

## 7. Competition (Why you and not them?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Honest competitive landscape. Clear, sustainable moat. Acknowledges strengths of competitors. |
| 4 | Good landscape analysis. Differentiation is clear but moat could be stronger. |
| 3 | Acknowledges competitors but differentiation is "better UX" or "more features." |
| 2 | Dismissive of competition. "We have no direct competitors." |
| 1 | No competitive analysis, or clearly inaccurate positioning. |

**Red flag:** "No direct competitors" almost always means insufficient research, not a unique market.

## 8. Team (Can you pull this off?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Deep domain expertise. Previous exits or relevant senior roles. Clear founder-market fit story. |
| 4 | Relevant experience, some gaps acknowledged with hiring plan. |
| 3 | General tech/business background but limited domain expertise. |
| 2 | Team credentials listed without connection to the opportunity. |
| 1 | No team slide, or team clearly lacks relevant experience. |

**Common mistake:** Listing impressive credentials (ex-Google, Stanford MBA) without explaining why those credentials matter for THIS specific problem.

## 9. Financials (Do the numbers make sense?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | 3-year projections with clear assumptions stated. Realistic growth tied to specific drivers. |
| 4 | Projections present with most assumptions stated. Growth is ambitious but defensible. |
| 3 | Projections present but assumptions unclear. Hockey stick without explanation. |
| 2 | Vague financial references. "We expect to be profitable by Year 3." |
| 1 | No financials, or obviously unrealistic projections. |

**Common mistake:** Hockey stick growth chart with no explanation of what causes the inflection. Every hockey stick needs a labeled catalyst.

## 10. The Ask (What do you need and why?)

| Score | Criteria |
|-------|---------|
| 5 | Clear round size, use of funds breakdown, specific milestones this funding enables. |
| 4 | Round size and use of funds clear. Milestones could be more specific. |
| 3 | Round size stated but use of funds is vague ("growth and hiring"). |
| 2 | Just a number with no context on how it will be used. |
| 1 | No ask, or ask is disconnected from the rest of the deck. |

**Common mistake:** "Use of funds: 40% engineering, 30% sales, 30% operations" — these percentages mean nothing without connecting them to specific milestones and outcomes.
