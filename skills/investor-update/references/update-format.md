# Investor Update Format Guide

## Complete Example

```
Subject: Acme Inc — March 2026 Investor Update

Hi everyone,

## TL;DR
Strong month. ARR crossed $2M for the first time (+12% MoM). Closed 5 new enterprise accounts. Burn remains stable at $180K/mo with 14 months runway.

## Key Metrics

| Metric | Mar 2026 | Feb 2026 | Change |
|--------|----------|----------|--------|
| ARR | $2.1M | $1.87M | +12% |
| MRR | $175K | $156K | +12% |
| Customers | 63 | 58 | +5 |
| Logo Churn | 1 | 2 | -50% |
| Monthly Burn | $180K | $175K | +3% |
| Runway | 14 mo | 15 mo | -1 mo |
| Team Size | 22 | 21 | +1 |

## Highlights
- Closed Meridian Capital ($45K ACV) — largest deal to date, referred by existing client
- Launched automated portfolio rebalancing — 3 clients in beta, 40% reduction in manual work
- Hired VP Engineering (Jane Smith, ex-Stripe) — starts April 7

## Challenges
- Sales cycle lengthening for enterprise (avg 47 days, up from 35). Investigating whether this is seasonal or structural.
- Two mid-market clients delayed onboarding due to internal compliance reviews. Revenue impact: ~$8K MRR delayed to Q2.

## Asks
1. Intro to Sarah Chen (CTO, BlueFund) — they're evaluating wealth platforms and match our ICP perfectly
2. Referrals to senior backend engineers — we have 2 open reqs and pipeline is thin

## What's Next
- Launch self-serve onboarding for SMB tier (target: April 15)
- Close 3 pipeline deals in negotiation stage ($120K combined ACV)
- Complete SOC 2 Type II audit

Best,
[Name]
```

## Subject Line Patterns

Good patterns:
- `[Company] — March 2026 Investor Update`
- `[Company] Monthly Update — March 2026`
- `[Company] Q1 2026 Update`

Avoid:
- `Quick update from [Company]` — too casual
- `[Company] — Exciting news!` — clickbait
- No subject line structure at all

## Metric Table Best Practices

- Always include MoM or QoQ change column
- Use percentages for rates, absolute numbers for counts
- Highlight the most important metric with a callout
- Include runway — investors always want to know
- If a metric looks bad, don't hide it — explain it in Challenges

## Tone Calibration

**Good month:** Lead with the win, but stay grounded. Not "We crushed it!" but "Strong month — ARR grew 12% driven by enterprise adoption."

**Bad month:** Lead with honesty, follow with the plan. Not "Things are tough" but "Growth slowed to 3% this month. Root cause: delayed enterprise deals. Here's our plan..."

**Fundraising update:** If actively raising, include a brief status. "Met with 8 firms, 3 in due diligence, targeting close by end of May." If not raising, don't mention it.

## Frequency

- Monthly is standard for early stage (pre-Series B)
- Quarterly is acceptable for later stage
- Always send on a consistent schedule (e.g., first Monday of the month)
- Late is worse than bad news — set expectations and meet them
