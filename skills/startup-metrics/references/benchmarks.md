# SaaS Metrics Benchmarks by Stage

Reference benchmarks for evaluating startup metrics. Based on industry data from OpenView, Bessemer, and SaaStr.

## Growth

| Metric | Early (<$1M ARR) | Growth ($1-10M ARR) | Scale (>$10M ARR) |
|--------|------------------|--------------------|--------------------|
| MoM Growth | >15% | >10% | >5% |
| YoY Growth | >3x | >2x | >50% |
| Net New MRR trend | Accelerating | Steady or accelerating | Steady |

## Retention

| Metric | Early | Growth | Scale |
|--------|-------|--------|-------|
| Gross Revenue Churn (monthly) | <5% | <3% | <2% |
| Net Revenue Retention (annual) | >100% | >110% | >120% |
| Logo Retention (annual) | >80% | >85% | >90% |
| Quick Ratio | >4x | >3x | >2x |

**Context:** NRR >130% is exceptional (Snowflake, Twilio-class). NRR <100% means the customer base is shrinking — existential for SaaS.

## Unit Economics

| Metric | Healthy | Good | Excellent |
|--------|---------|------|-----------|
| LTV:CAC | >3x | >5x | >8x |
| CAC Payback | <18 months | <12 months | <6 months |
| Gross Margin | >60% | >70% | >80% |

**Context:** LTV:CAC <1x means losing money on every customer. <3x is concerning for most VCs. Very high LTV:CAC (>10x) may indicate under-investment in growth.

## Efficiency

| Metric | Concerning | Healthy | Excellent |
|--------|-----------|---------|-----------|
| Burn Multiple | >3x | 1-2x | <1x |
| Runway | <6 months | 12-18 months | >24 months |
| Magic Number | <0.5 | 0.5-1.0 | >1.0 |
| Rule of 40 | <20% | 20-40% | >40% |

**Context:** Burn multiple is the single best efficiency metric (per David Sacks). <1x means generating more ARR than burning cash. Magic Number >1.0 signals to increase S&M investment.

## How to Use These Benchmarks

- Compare the user's metrics against the column matching their stage
- Flag metrics in the "Concerning" range with specific improvement suggestions
- Celebrate metrics in the "Excellent" range
- Always note that benchmarks vary by industry and business model — these are SaaS medians, not universal rules
- Enterprise SaaS typically has higher churn but higher ACV; SMB SaaS has lower churn but lower ACV
